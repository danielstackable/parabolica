BEng Electrical, Electronic and Information Engineering

Nagoya University's Electrical, Electronic and Information Engineering Program is part of the university’s broader Automotive Engineering curriculum, designed to train future leaders in Japan’s globally renowned automotive sector. This program blends advanced coursework in electrical and electronic systems with hands-on, industry-relevant applications tied to modern vehicle design, intelligent transportation, and sustainable mobility. Students begin by building a strong foundation in engineering physics and mathematics, before progressing to applied subjects such as electromagnetism, power electronics, signal processing, materials science, and automotive software engineering. Emphasis is placed on emerging CASE technologies—Connected, Autonomous, Shared, and Electric—through both lab work and seminars led by industry experts. Coursework is complemented by optional internships during academic breaks, offering direct experience with real-world automotive innovation. In their fourth year, students join a faculty-led research group, gaining practical research experience in areas such as vehicle control systems, intelligent mobility, and energy-efficient materials. This program is ideal for students aiming to work at the intersection of electrical systems, software, and automotive technology. Graduates are well-equipped for roles in Japan's automotive industry or in global R&D, transportation, and energy sectors.
