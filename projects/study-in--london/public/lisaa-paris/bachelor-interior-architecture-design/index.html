Bachelor in Interior Architecture & Design

The Interior Architecture & Design program at LISAA Paris is a five-year integrated course that awards a Bachelor’s degree after the third year, followed by two years at the Master's level (Years 4 and 5) in line with the French higher education model. Students begin with a foundational year, progressing through advanced design training and professional immersion. The curriculum develops both creative and managerial skills, enabling graduates to: Analyze a client brief and develop a structured interior design proposal. Incorporate trends and innovation to create meaningful spatial solutions. Oversee project execution and coordinate suppliers and service providers. Specializations in Years 4 and 5 include global design, hospitality, sustainable design, scenography, and UX & interactive environments. The course is available in French and English, with mandatory internships in Years 3 and 4. The final year may be completed as a sandwich course (alternance), combining paid employment with study. Graduates earn a Level 7 RNCP certification, recognized across Europe, and are prepared for careers in interior architecture, spatial design, scenography, and user-centered experiential environments. Also offered at LISAA campuses in Rennes, Nantes, Strasbourg, Bordeaux, Toulouse, and Montpellier.

LISAA School of Art & Design, (L'Institut Supérieur des Arts Appliqués) is a French private college for applied arts education founded in 1986. LISAA has locations in Paris, Rennes, Nantes, Strasbourg, Bordeaux and Toulouse. The school is one of about 100 recognized by the French Ministry of Culture and Communication. Diplomas are offered in graphic design, animation & video games, interior architecture & design, and fashion
